## 1. What is Afro Genie?

Afro Genie lets you:

- **Search** for African songs, artists, and genres.
- **Read lyrics** in the original language and in translation (e.g. English).
- **Understand context** with cultural notes and explanations.
- **Request** translations for songs that don’t have lyrics or translations yet.
- **Join the community** to discuss songs and share knowledge.
- **Explore** by language, genre, and artist.

You can use most features without an account. Signing in lets you save favorites, join the community, and (for artists) use the artist dashboard.

## 2. How to access the app

- **Live site:** [https://afrogenie.web.app](https://afrogenie.web.app)
- Works in any modern browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge) on **phone, tablet, or computer**.

Open the link and you’ll land on the **Home** page.

## 3. Getting started (no account required)

1. Open **https://afrogenie.web.app**.
2. On **Home** you’ll see:
   - A search bar (top of the page and in the header).
   - Shortcuts: **Join Community**, **For Artists**.
   - Sections: Popular Songs, Languages, Top Artists, Genres, Trending Discussions.
3. Use the **header** (top of every page):
   - **AfroGenie** logo: click to go back to Home.
   - **Search**: type an artist, song, or genre and press Enter or pick a suggestion.
   - **Menu** (on mobile): tap to open links (Home, Browse Songs, Search, Community, Request Translation, For Artists).
   - **Sign In** or your **profile** (after sign-in): account and profile options.

You can search, open songs, read lyrics/translations, and browse the catalog **without signing in**.

---

## 4. Searching for songs and artists

- **From Home:** Type in the main search bar and press Enter (or choose a suggestion).
- **From the header:** Use the search field; suggestions appear as you type (artists, songs, genres, lyrics).
- **From the menu (mobile):** Tap **Search** to open the search page, then type your query.

**Search page:**

- Enter a song title, artist name, or genre.
- Results are grouped into **Songs**, **Artists**, and **Genres**.
- Tap a song to open its **lyrics/translation page**.
- Tap an artist to open their **artist page** (songs, bio, link to Spotify if available).
- If there are **no results**, you can **Request song** so the team can add it later.

---

## 5. Viewing lyrics and translations

1. From search or catalog, open a **song** (tap the song title/card).
2. You’ll see the **song page** with:
   - Song title and artist.
   - **Original lyrics** (when available).
   - **Translation** (when available).
   - **Cultural context** (when added by the team).
   - **View options**: e.g. Tabs, Side-by-Side, Top & Bottom (use the “View” dropdown).
   - **Font size**: use the “Font” slider to make text larger or smaller.
3. **Actions** (often at the bottom or in the toolbar):
   - **Like**: add to your favorites (requires sign-in).
   - **Share**: share the song page link.
   - **Discuss**: open community to talk about this song.
4. If the song has **no lyrics or translation yet**, you’ll see a **Request Translation** (or similar) button to ask the team to add it.

---

## 6. Browsing the song catalog

- **Header or menu:** Open **Browse Songs** (or **Songs Catalog**).
- You get a **filterable list** of all songs:
  - **Search** within the catalog (by title, artist, genre).
  - **Filters:** Artist, Genre, Year, Language.
  - **Sort:** e.g. Popularity, Title, Artist, Year.
- On **mobile**, use the **“Filters & Sort”** button to show or hide filters.
- Tap a song row to go to its **lyrics/translation page**.
- Use **Previous/Next** at the bottom to move between pages if the list is long.

---

## 7. Requesting a translation

You can ask for a song to be added or translated in two ways:

**Option A — From a song page (recommended)**

1. Open a **song** that has no lyrics or no translation.
2. On that page, tap **Request Translation** (or similar).
3. Your request is sent to the team; they may add lyrics or a translation later.

**Option B — From the Request Translation page**

1. In the header or footer, open **Request Translation**.
2. The app may first redirect you to Home with a short message.
3. If you see the **Request Translation** form:
   - Enter **Artist** and **Song title** (and optional lyrics).
   - Choose **Original language** and **Translate to**.
   - Tap **Get AI Translation** to get an instant AI-generated translation and context (for testing/draft).
   - If you’re signed in, you can **Save Translation**; otherwise you may be asked to sign in.

Saved or requested translations are reviewed by the team; human-added cultural context may be added later.

## 8. Community forum

- **Go to Community:** Header or menu → **Community** (or **Join Community** on Home).
- **What you can do:**
  - Browse **categories** and **topics** (discussions).
  - **Read** posts and replies.
  - **Create a topic** (e.g. from **Create Topic** or from a song’s **Discuss** button).
- **Sign-in:** Usually required to post; you may be able to read some content without an account.
- From a **song page**, **Discuss** takes you to the community with that song in context (if supported).

---

## 9. Creating an account and signing in

- Tap **Sign In** in the header.
- **Sign-in options:**
  - **Email and password:** Enter email and password, then **Sign In**.
  - **Create account:** Switch to “Sign up”, enter display name, email, and password, then **Create Account**.
  - **Google:** Use **Continue with Google**.
  - **Spotify:** Use **Sign in with Spotify** (redirects to Spotify and back).
- After sign-in you can:
  - **Like** songs (favorites).
  - **Post** in the community.
  - **Save** or **request** translations (where available).
  - **Artist dashboard** (if you registered as an artist).

Use the **profile/avatar** in the header to open **Profile & Community**, **Request Translation**, **Artist Dashboard** (if applicable), and **Logout**.

---

## 10. For artists

- **Artist signup:** Header or menu → **For Artists** (or **Artist signup**).
- Register with your **stage name**, **genre**, **bio**, and optional links.
- After approval (if applicable), sign in and open **Artist Dashboard** from the profile menu.
- The dashboard is for artists to manage their presence and see stats (exact features depend on the current build).

---

## 11. Mobile and desktop

- The app is **responsive**: same features on **phone**, **tablet**, and **desktop**.
- **On mobile:**
  - Use the **menu** (hamburger icon) for main links.
  - **Search** is in the header (sometimes under the menu on small screens).
  - Buttons and links are sized for touch (e.g. 44px targets).
  - On song pages, **View** and **Font** controls are usable on small screens; the **bottom action bar** (Like, Share, Discuss) respects safe areas on notched devices.
- **On desktop:** Full layout with sidebars and filters visible where applicable.
